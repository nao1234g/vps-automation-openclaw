## 1. Japan: The First Regulated Yen Stablecoin

### JPYC -- The Pioneer
On October 27, 2025, Japan launched **JPYC** -- the country's first fully regulated yen-pegged stablecoin under the revised Payment Services Act.

| Detail | JPYC |
|---|---|
| Issuer | JPYC Inc. (Tokyo) |
| License | FSA-approved funds-transfer service provider |
| Backing | 1:1 JPY in domestic bank deposits + JGBs |
| Chains | Ethereum, Polygon |
| Target | ¥10 trillion (~$65B) in circulation within 3 years |
| Revenue | No transaction fees; earns from JGB interest |
| Unique feature | Fraud compensation policy |
| Limitation | Japanese residents only |

### The Competition
| Stablecoin | Issuer | Status | Market |
|---|---|---|---|
| **JPYC** | JPYC Inc. | ✅ Live (Oct 2025) | Japan only |
| **GYEN** | GMO-Z (NY Trust) | ✅ Live (since 2021) | US-regulated |
| **JPYR** | Offshore | ✅ Live | Global (avoids FSA) |

### Coming Soon
- **SMBC**: Trust-based coin model in development
- **Japan Post Bank**: Deposit tokens by 2026 using DeCurret DCP infrastructure
- **Monex**: Remittance-focused stablecoin

**Signal:** Japan's megabanks are entering the game. When MUFG, SMBC, and Mizuho all issue yen stablecoins, the market dynamics shift dramatically. JPYC's 3-year target of ¥10 trillion is ambitious but signals institutional confidence.

## 2. South Korea: The Political Deadlock

Korea's stablecoin story is a turf war between two regulators.

### Bank of Korea (BOK) vs. Financial Services Commission (FSC)

| Position | BOK | FSC |
|---|---|---|
| Who can issue stablecoins? | Only banks (51% ownership rule) | Banks + fintech companies |
| Reasoning | Solvency, AML, monetary control | Competition, innovation, global precedent |
| Model | Conservative (bank-only) | Open (EU/Japan-style) |

This fight has **delayed the entire Digital Asset Basic Act (DABA) Phase 2** from 2025 to 2026. President Lee Jae-myung's ruling party committed to introducing a bill by end of January 2026.

### Proposed Rules (If DABA Passes)
- 100% reserves in low-risk instruments (bank deposits or government bonds)
- Reserves under independent custodians
- Foreign stablecoins (USDC, USDT) allowed **only if** the issuer has a licensed local branch/subsidiary
- Interest-bearing stablecoins: still under debate

### KRW Stablecoin Development
- **KakaoBank**: Developing KRW-pegged stablecoin on Kaia blockchain
- **Project Unify** (LINE NEXT + Kaia): Multi-currency super-app supporting KRW, JPY, THB, IDR, PHP, MYR, SGD, USD

**Signal:** The deadlock won't last forever. When it breaks, Korea's 10.17 million crypto users will get access to regulated stablecoins overnight. The infrastructure (130M LINE/Kakao wallets) is ready. The regulation isn't.

## 3. Hong Kong: Licensing Under Pressure

### The Framework
Hong Kong's **Stablecoins Ordinance** passed May 21, 2025 and took effect August 1, 2025. Stablecoin issuance is now under HKMA supervision.

**Key requirement:** Every stablecoin holder must undergo **KYC**. This is unusual globally and raises questions about DeFi compatibility.

### March 2026: First Licenses

| Detail | Status |
|---|---|
| Applications received | 36 (from 40+ expressions of interest) |
| First licenses | March 2026 |
| Expected number | "Very few" -- cautious rollout |
| Assessment criteria | Use cases, reserve backing, risk management, AML |

### Notable Applicants

| **Anchorpoint Financial** | Standard Chartered + Animoca Brands + HKT | HKD-backed |
| **Ant Group** | Alibaba | Under review |
| **Bank of China HK** | State-owned | Under review |
| **HSBC** | Global bank | Signaled intention |
| **ICBC** | State-owned | Signaled intention |

### The Beijing Problem (February 2026)

This is the biggest story nobody in the West is covering.

In February 2026, **eight Chinese state regulators jointly reaffirmed the ban on crypto activities**, singling out stablecoins as a priority enforcement area.

**Beijing's concerns:**
1. Loss of monetary control over renminbi instruments
2. "Dollarization of the digital asset economy" via USD-pegged stablecoins
3. Stablecoins used by organized crime (up to $44 million moved daily)

**Hong Kong's response:** Pressing ahead anyway. The "one country, two systems" framework is being stress-tested in real-time.

**Signal:** If Hong Kong successfully issues stablecoin licenses despite Beijing's objections, it becomes the most important regulatory precedent in Asian crypto history. If Beijing intervenes, the entire HK crypto ecosystem faces an existential threat.

## 4. Singapore: The Quiet Leader

### MAS Stablecoin Framework
Finalized August 2023. Expected to become law mid-2026.

**Rules:**
- Applies to single-currency stablecoins pegged to SGD or G10 currencies
- **100% reserve requirement**
- Bi-monthly independent audits
- Redemption within 5 business days
- Only MAS-regulated stablecoins can be marketed as "stablecoins"

### Licensed Issuers

| Issuer | Stablecoins | Reserves | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **StraitsX** | XSGD, XUSD | DBS + Standard Chartered | Licensed MPI, listed on Coinbase (Oct 2025) |
| **Paxos** | USDP, others | Full MAS approval | First to gain full approval |

StraitsX plans to expand to Solana by early 2026.

### Non-USD Stablecoins Growing
In Q2 2025, **258,000 transactions** of non-USD stablecoins in Southeast Asia. SGD-pegged tokens made up **70.1%** of that market.

**Tokenized MAS Bills:** MAS announced trials for 2026 -- the government itself issuing tokenized instruments.

**Signal:** Singapore isn't trying to be the biggest. It's trying to be the most trusted. That's a long-term play that appeals to institutions managing billions.

## 5. USDT vs. USDC: The Asia Battleground

|---|---|---|
| USDT (Tether) | ~60% | $13.3 trillion |
| USDC (Circle) | ~25% | $18.3 trillion |
| Others | ~15% | — |

USDC processed more volume despite lower market cap -- driven by institutional and programmatic usage.

### Asia-Specific Dynamics

**USDT dominates Asia:**
- Primary dollar substitute in markets where USD access is restricted (especially China)
- Offshore USDT trading is massive and largely unregulated
- MiCA delisted USDT from EU exchanges (March 2025), pushing more USDT activity to Asia-Pacific

**USDC is expanding into Southeast Asia:**
- Circle gained EU advantage through MiCA compliance
- Now pursuing Singapore and Hong Kong licenses
- Institutional preference in regulated markets

### Regulatory Requirements for Foreign Stablecoins

| Country | Rule |
|---|---|
| Japan | Must comply with Payment Services Act |
| Korea | Issuer must have a licensed local branch |
| Hong Kong | KYC on every holder |
| Singapore | Must be MAS-regulated to be called "stablecoin" |

**Signal:** The era of borderless, unregulated stablecoin usage in Asia is ending. Every major market is building walls. Circle (USDC) is better positioned for this regulated future than Tether (USDT).
